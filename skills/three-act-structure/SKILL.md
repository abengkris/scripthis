# Skill: Three-Act Structure

The **Three-Act Structure** is the foundational architecture of storytelling, dividing a narrative into three parts:

## 1. The Setup (Act I)
*   **The Status Quo:** Establish the protagonist's normal world.
*   **The Inciting Incident:** An event that disrupts the life of the protagonist.
*   **The Plot Point 1:** The protagonist makes a choice that forces them into the new world of the story.

## 2. The Confrontation (Act II)
*   **Rising Action:** Obstacles increase as the protagonist struggles toward their goal.
*   **The Midpoint:** A major shift occurs, raising the stakes or changing the goal.
*   **The Low Point:** The protagonist suffers a significant setback or defeat.

## 3. The Resolution (Act III)
*   **The Climax:** The final, most intense confrontation between the protagonist and their conflict.
*   **The Falling Action:** Resolving lingering plot threads.
*   **The Resolution:** The "new normal" established after the journey.

---

### How to use this skill
When the user asks for structure, apply these stages to:
- Evaluate the pacing of the current draft.
- Check if the climax provides emotional closure to the setup.
- Identify if the midpoint shift is strong enough to carry the Act II tension.
