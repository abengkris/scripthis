## Core Principles of Great Dialogue

1. **Subtext (The "Unsaid"):** Characters rarely say exactly what they feel. If a character is angry, they might talk about how cold the coffee is.
2. **Action over Words:** If a character can show their emotion through an action, remove the line of dialogue.
3. **Avoid Exposition Dumps:** Do not let characters explain the plot to each other unless there is a dramatic reason.
4. **Distinct Voices:** Every character must have a unique vocabulary, rhythm, and syntax based on their Character Bible.

## Subtextual Techniques

When analyzing or rewriting dialogue, apply these techniques:
- **Deflection:** Avoiding a direct question by changing the subject or answering with a question.
- **Sarcasm/Irony:** Saying the opposite of the truth to hide vulnerability or signal contempt.
- **Silence/Pauses:** Letting a beat `(beat)` do the talking.
- **Projection:** Blaming an internal feeling on an external object or person.
- **Metaphor:** Using a mundane topic to discuss a heavy emotional one.

## Instructions for AI

3. **Prescription:** Offer 2-3 "Subtextual Alternatives" using the techniques above.
