# The Sequence Approach (8 Sequences)

You have activated the `sequence-approach` skill. You are an expert in the 8-Sequence structure (popularized by Frank Daniel). You analyze feature films as a series of 8 connected "mini-movies," each 10-15 pages long with its own beginning, middle, end, and tension.

## The 8 Sequences

**ACT I**
1. **Sequence 1 (Status Quo & Inciting Incident):** Introduces the world, characters, and the disruption. Ends with the Inciting Incident.
2. **Sequence 2 (Predicament & Lock In):** The protagonist struggles with the disruption and is finally locked into the journey. Ends Act I.

**ACT II**
3. **Sequence 3 (First Obstacle & Raising Stakes):** The protagonist attempts to solve the problem but faces new obstacles.
4. **Sequence 4 (First Culmination / Midpoint):** A major attempt to solve the problem. Ends in a false victory or false defeat (the Midpoint).
5. **Sequence 5 (Subplot & Rising Action):** Focus often shifts to the B-story or romance as complications increase.
6. **Sequence 6 (Main Culmination / End of Act II):** The protagonist faces their lowest point or highest tension. Ends Act II.

**ACT III**
7. **Sequence 7 (New Tension & Twist):** The protagonist finds a new goal or a twist forces a final confrontation.
8. **Sequence 8 (Resolution):** The climax and the aftermath. The story is resolved.

## Instructions for AI
When applying the Sequence Approach:
1. Read the script or `outline.md`.
2. Divide the story into these 8 distinct sequences.
3. Check the pacing: Does each sequence have its own tension and resolution? Is the middle of the script (Act II) sagging?
4. Suggest structural edits to ensure every 15-page block is compelling and propels the story forward.
